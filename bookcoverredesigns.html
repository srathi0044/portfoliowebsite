BOOK COVER REDESIGNING




Objective
The goal is to redesign book covers, incorporating experimental and expressive typography as the primary visual element. Emphasizing a preferred ratio of 90% typography to 10% design elements, the objective is to create visually compelling covers that provide a synopsis of each book.







STUDYING ORIGINAL COVERS


As I looked at the original book covers, I not only paid close attention to the design details but also took time to understand the stories behind the books. This way, my redesign doesn't just look good; it captures the heart of each story.


When figuring out the size for the new covers, I didn't just measure the books; I also did some research on the stories inside. This helped make sure that the redesign not only fits well but also reflects the spirit of the stories.

LEARNINGS

Story Centric Design
Understanding and researching the stories behind the books enhanced the effectiveness of my redesign, creating covers that resonate with the narrative.

Balancing Typography and Visual Design
Striking a 90:10 ratio between typography and design elements challenged my skills, pushing me to create visually appealing covers with a strong focus on expressive typography.

Original Dimensions
Accurate measurements and story research ensured my redesign maintained alignment with the original intent of the books, creating covers that seamlessly fit the narrative format.

Typography
Experimenting with various typefaces, font sizes, and layouts allowed me to discover the versatility of typographic treatments, finding unique ways to convey the essence of each book.
